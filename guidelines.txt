QNT 730: Business Data Mining
Term Project Guidelines
Prof. Reuben B. Marfo | Spring 2026
1. Overview
The term project is a major component of this course and is designed to give you hands-on experience
applying data mining and machine learning techniques to real business problems. You will
work in teams (2 students unless otherwise approved). This project requires technical rigor, clear
business framing, and professional presentation.
2. Project Timeline & Requirements
2.1 Step 1: Topic & Dataset Selection
Due: (02/23)
Each team must:
• Identify a clearly defined business problem
• Select an appropriate and reasonably sized dataset
• Post a brief description (1 paragraph) via the Blackboard discussion board
You are encouraged to use publicly available datasets (Kaggle, UCI, government data, etc.) or
work-related datasets (if permitted and anonymized). Ensure you understand the business context
behind the data.
2.2 Step 2: Project Proposal
Due: (03/30)
Submit a 1–2 page proposal including:
1. Business Problem Statement
2. Dataset Description
3. Proposed Data Mining Techniques (e.g., classification, clustering, regression, association
rules, neural networks)
4. Evaluation Plan
• Metrics (e.g., Accuracy, AUC, F1, RMSE, MAE)
• Validation strategy (Train/Test split, Cross-validation)
5. At least two academic or practitioner references
Note: You are strongly encouraged to discuss your proposal with me before submitting.

2.3 Step 3: Final Term Paper
Due: ( 04/30)
Submit one final paper per team.
Paper Requirements
• Length: 15–20 pages, 1.5 spacing
• Professional formatting and writing
• Must include the sections below (as applicable):
– Abstract
– Introduction & Business Context
– Literature Review / Related Work
– Data & Preprocessing
– Methodology (models, features, training procedure)
– Results & Model Comparison
– Model Evaluation & Validation
– Business Implications (what decisions change based on results)
– Limitations
– Conclusion
– References
• Include well-labeled figures and tables
• Include a reproducibility appendix (code, parameters, and instructions)
If your project includes a dashboard, deployed model, GitHub repository, or HTML-based interface,
include a link in your submission.
3. Grading Breakdown
• Project Proposal: 20%
• Final Term Paper: 70%
• Peer Evaluation: 10%
Each student must email confidential feedback on their teammates by the last day of class. I reserve
the right to adjust individual grades based on contribution.
4. Project Types
4.1 Option A: Applied Business Data Project
Address a real business problem using data mining techniques. Example topics include:

• Customer churn prediction
• Credit risk modeling
• Fraud detection
• Customer segmentation
• Recommendation systems
• Sales forecasting
• Text mining / sentiment analysis
4.2 Option B: Replication & Extension Study
Replicate a published study (journal article or textbook case) and extend it using:
• A new dataset
• A different modeling technique
• Improved validation methods
• Comparative model evaluation
Your final report should follow the structure of a professional analytics paper.
5. Technical Expectations
I expect:
• Clean data preprocessing and documentation
• Feature engineering
• Comparison of at least two modeling approaches
• Proper validation (holdout and/or cross-validation as appropriate)
• Interpretation of results (not just model output)
• Clear connection between analytics and business decision-making
This is not just a coding exercise; it is an analytics problem-solving exercise.
6. Tools
You may use:
• Python (preferred)
• R
• SQL
• Tableau / Power BI (if appropriate)
• Jupyter Notebook (recommended)

7. Academic Integrity
All work must be original. You may reference external resources but must properly cite them.

or critical thinking.
Submission: All submissions will be made via Blackboard (unless otherwise announced).
